The archive as a public room

Good editorial design lowers the temperature around the text. It gives each paragraph enough space to breathe without turning the page into a museum object.

Reading systems

A publication system needs durable defaults for headings, lists, quotations, links, and inline code snippets. Those defaults should work before an editor thinks about them.

  • Serif headings establish academic rhythm.
  • Slate body text reduces contrast fatigue.
  • Measured widths keep the line length readable.

Reading with attention

The best publication pages do not compete with the argument. They create a room around the reader: quiet margins, predictable rhythm, useful signposts, and a sense that the text has been cared for.

A compact editorial checklist

Before an essay is published, the system should already support hierarchy, citation, quotation, and movement between sections.

  • Give headings a clear scale and generous spacing.
  • Keep metadata visible without making it loud.
  • Use links as invitations rather than interruptions.
Careful design makes careful reading feel possible.

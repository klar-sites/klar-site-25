Comprehensive care pathways designed to restore function and eliminate pain.

Understanding Shoulder Injuries

The shoulder is the most mobile joint in the human body, making it highly susceptible to injuries. Whether caused by acute trauma, repetitive overhead movements, or age-related degeneration, shoulder conditions can significantly impact daily life.

At Nordic Shoulder Clinic, we specialize in diagnosing and treating the full spectrum of shoulder pathology. Our goal is to reduce pain, restore mechanics, and return you to your highest level of function.

Symptoms & Causes

Recognizing the signs early can lead to more effective treatment.

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent or sharp pain during movement
  • Stiffness and loss of range of motion
  • Weakness in the arm or shoulder
  • Clicking, popping, or grinding sensations
  • Difficulty sleeping on the affected side

Primary Causes

  • Sports injuries (throwing, swimming, contact sports)
  • Repetitive overhead motions (occupational)
  • Acute trauma (falls, accidents)
  • Degenerative wear and tear over time
  • Poor posture and muscular imbalances

Diagnostic Methods

Accurate diagnosis is the foundation of effective treatment.

Physical Exam

Comprehensive orthopedic tests to assess range of motion, strength, and instability.

Ultrasound

Dynamic imaging to evaluate tendons and bursae in real-time during movement.

MRI

Detailed imaging of soft tissues to detect labral tears, tendon ruptures, and inflammation.

X-Ray

Quick assessment of bone structures to rule out fractures or severe arthritis.

Treatment Options

We offer a full spectrum of treatments, from conservative care to advanced surgical interventions.

Conservative Care

Non-invasive approaches to healing.

  • Targeted Physiotherapy
  • Manual Therapy & Mobilization
  • Activity Modification
  • Therapeutic Exercises

Interventional

Advanced pain management solutions.

  • Corticosteroid Injections
  • Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP)
  • Hydrodilatation
  • Nerve Blocks

Surgical

Minimally invasive orthopedic surgery.

  • Arthroscopic Rotator Cuff Repair
  • Labral Repair (SLAP/Bankart)
  • Shoulder Stabilization
  • Total Shoulder Replacement

Rehabilitation Process

A structured, phased approach to ensure a safe and durable recovery.

Phase 1: Protection & Pain Relief

Focuses on reducing inflammation, protecting the injured tissue, and maintaining basic mobility without aggravating the condition.

Phase 2: Mobility Restoration

Gentle stretching and manual therapy to restore full, pain-free range of motion and reverse stiffness.

Phase 3: Strength & Endurance

Progressive loading exercises to rebuild muscle strength around the shoulder girdle, focusing on the rotator cuff and scapular stabilizers.

Phase 4: Function & Return to Sport

Sport-specific or work-specific training to ensure the shoulder can handle the demands of daily life and athletic activity safely.

Recovery Expectations

What to expect on your journey back to full health.

2-4 Weeks

Noticeable reduction in acute pain and inflammation. Improved ease of daily activities.

6-12 Weeks

Significant restoration of range of motion and beginning of strength rebuilding.

3-6 Months

Full functional recovery for most conditions. Return to sports or heavy labor with confidence.
